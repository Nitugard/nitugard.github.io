Introduction

Since the dawn of time man was fascinated with prime numbers. That tradition continues and we too stand in awe, so much so, that we won't speak about these almighty numbers.

What is modular aritmethic? Modular aritmethic is based upon modulus operator. Modulus operator returns remainder after division by integer.

$$a=q_1 * n + r\\ {a}\mod{(n)} = r$$

What is congruence? Congruence, in agreement lat. relates numbers based on their remainder. Numbers are said to be congruent modulo N when their remainder are equal.

$$a\equiv{b\mod(n)}$$

If two numbers form congruence, then their difference is a number that is some multiple of n. Proof:

$$a=q_1 * n + r\\ b=q_2 * n + r\\ a-b = n(q_1-q_2)=qn$$

What is congruence class? Set of every integer that forms congruence. Congruence class is sometimes called residue class.

What is least residue? Least reasidue is smallest integer that forms congruence.

$$a\equiv{b_{min}\mod(n)}$$

Properties

Modulus operator is only defined for integers including positive and negative. Therefore arithmetic properties must also obey this limit.

The congruence relation satisfies all the conditions of the equivalence relation. Base properties are listed below:

  1. Reflextivity \( a\equiv{a\mod{n}} \)

  2. Symmetry \(a\equiv{b\mod{n}} \; b\equiv{a\mod{n}} \)

  3. Transitivity \( a\equiv{b\mod{n}} \ b\equiv{c\mod{n}} \ a\equiv{c\mod{n}} \)

  4. Translation \( a + k\equiv{b + k\mod{n}} \)

  5. Scaling \( a*k\equiv{b*k\mod{n}} \)

  6. Addition \(a\equiv{b\mod{n}} \\ c\equiv{d\mod{n}} \\ a+c\equiv{b+d\mod{n}} \\ a-c\equiv{b-d\mod{n}} \)

  7. Multiplication \(a\equiv{b\mod{n}}\\ c\equiv{d\mod{n}}\\ a*c\equiv{b*d\mod{n}} \)

  8. Exponentiation \( a^k\equiv{b^k\mod{n}}\ k>0 \)

  9. Polynomial \( p(a)\equiv{p(b)\mod{n}} \)
