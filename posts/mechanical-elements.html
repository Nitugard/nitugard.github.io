Introduction

Bearings

Bearings support shafts and axles by reducing their motion, radial and/or axial, while better defining their position.

  1. Friction bearings
  2. Rolling bearings
  3. Magnetic bearings

Lubricated bearings offer protection from:

  1. Excess friction
  2. Inpurities
  3. Corrosion
  4. Noise, vibration
  5. High temperatures

Fluid bearings are bearings in which the load is supported by a thin layer of rapidly moving pressurized liquid or gas between the bearing surfaces.

They can be broadly classified into two types: fluid dynamic bearings (also known as hydrodynamic bearings) and hydrostatic bearings.
